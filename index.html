Malaysia Permanent Forest Reserve Density and Log Production of Each State

Permanent Forest Reserve Area

Permanent Reserved Forests (PRFs) are government lands that have been gazetted under the National Forestry Act 1984. Under the National Forestry Policy 1978 (Amendment 1992), PRFs must be managed and classified under four main functions: protection, production, recreation, and research. Sarawak, with the densest permanent forest reserve area, is a legally protected area that ensures sustainable forest management and biodiversity conservation, by allowing controlled logging and community use as part of strict regulations. It plays a vital role in conserving Sarawak's rich ecosystems, including endangered species, and is part of larger initiatives such as the Heart of Borneo. Despite efforts towards certification and sustainable practices, issues such as illegal logging and land use conflicts persist.

Log Production

Based on the data, Despite its lush PRFs, the production of wood log out of Sarawak is the biggest contributor to the nation's log production. Altough, the production of year 2021 dropped significantly compared to the last 20 years. Log production is regulated heavily by the Sarawak Forestry Department to ensure sustainability of logging operations. Logging activities mainly take place in designated areas such as permanent forest reserves and licensed logging concessions.Despite regulatory efforts, the industry continues to face challenges such as over-harvesting, illegal logging, and concerns about deforestation and environmental sustainability.
